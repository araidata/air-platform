# AI Assurance Philosophy

AI assurance combines technical assessment, governance evidence, civil-rights review, security risk management, and operational accountability.

## Assurance Domains

- Security.
- Privacy.
- Bias and civil rights.
- Explainability.
- Governance evidence.
- Operational readiness.

## Assurance Is Evidence-Based

The platform should never present scores or approvals as vibes. Every meaningful assurance outcome should trace to:

- Findings.
- Scanner outputs.
- Manual review notes.
- Policies.
- Screenshots.
- Prompt/output samples.
- Configuration snapshots.
- Human review controls.
- Remediation records.
- Retest records.

## Assurance Is Workflow-Based

Assurance is not a single report. It is a lifecycle:

1. Inventory system.
2. Classify risk.
3. Collect evidence.
4. Run assessments.
5. Normalize findings.
6. Triage findings.
7. Route reviews.
8. Approve, block, or approve with exception.
9. Track remediation.
10. Retest.
11. Preserve history.

## Scanner Role

Scanners are evidence producers. They are not the governance system. The platform uses scanner output as input into human-understandable findings, evidence, scoring, and decisions.
