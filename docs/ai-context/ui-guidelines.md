# UI Guidelines

The UI must feel like an operational AI assurance center for county government.

## Desired Feel

- Authoritative.
- Operational.
- Calm.
- Trustworthy.
- Security-focused.
- Audit-friendly.
- Government-grade.

The closest references are CrowdStrike, Wiz, Microsoft Defender, Splunk, and ServiceNow rather than consumer AI chat products.

## Avoid

- Neon AI styling.
- Chatbot-first design.
- Toy-like dashboards.
- Flashy gimmicks.
- Vague AI magic language.
- Marketing hero sections.
- Low-density executive fluff.

## Use

- Dark professional dashboard.
- Clear cards for summary metrics.
- Risk heatmaps.
- Findings tables.
- Evidence views.
- Executive scorecards.
- Analyst drill-downs.
- Clean typography.
- Restrained colors.
- Readable density.

## First Pages

- Executive Dashboard.
- AI Inventory.
- Assessment Workspace.
- Findings Queue.
- System Detail Page.
- Bias & Civil Rights Dashboard.
- Security Findings Dashboard.
- Evidence & Audit Page.
- AI Review Board Queue.
- Reports Page.
- Settings / Integrations Page.

## Copy Tone

Use direct operational language:

- "Open findings"
- "Evidence missing"
- "Risk accepted"
- "Retest required"
- "Approval blocked"

Avoid vague phrases:

- "AI magic"
- "Supercharge governance"
- "Intelligent insights"
- "Ask your data"
