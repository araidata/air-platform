# Design System

The UI should feel like a serious operations center for public-sector AI assurance.

## Design Character

- Authoritative.
- Calm.
- Trustworthy.
- Security-focused.
- Audit-friendly.
- Government-grade.

The product should feel closer to CrowdStrike, Wiz, Microsoft Defender, Splunk, and ServiceNow than a chatbot app.

## Visual Direction

- Dark professional dashboard base.
- High-contrast text.
- Restrained color accents.
- Clear status colors.
- Dense but readable layouts.
- Tables and drill-downs as first-class surfaces.
- Cards only where they summarize or group operational records.

## Color Use

Use colors to communicate status:

- Critical/high risk.
- Medium risk.
- Low risk.
- Approved.
- Blocked.
- Needs review.
- Evidence missing.

Avoid decorative neon gradients, generic AI purple glow, and playful visuals.

## Typography

- Use clean, readable type.
- Keep headings compact inside operational surfaces.
- Avoid oversized hero text.
- Prioritize scanability.

## Components

Expected components:

- Risk score cards.
- Findings tables.
- System inventory table.
- Evidence drawer or detail panel.
- Review queue.
- Status badges.
- Filter bars.
- Framework mapping chips.
- Timeline for decisions and retests.

## Language

Use clear operational labels:

- Open findings.
- Approval blocked.
- Evidence missing.
- Risk accepted.
- Retest required.
- Awaiting review.

Avoid hype language and vague AI claims.
